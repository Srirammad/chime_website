    What do all checking account number mean?

    Your checking account includes two identifying sets of numbers: a routing number and an account number. A routing number is a nine-digit number that is used to identify a bank or financial institution when clearing funds for electronic transfers or processing checks in the United States. An account number identifies your specific account at your financial institution. To find the routing and account numbers for your Chime account, head to the Move Money section of your Chime Account and select Direct Deposit. You’ll be able to see the name of the bank that provides banking services for your Chime Checking Account, along with the routing number and checking account number.

    Will an online checking account build or hurt credit?

    A checking account typically has no positive or negative impact on your credit score. However, your credit can be affected if you receive an overdraft fee and don’t pay it. Once that fee goes to collections, it can damage your credit score. Avoiding credit entirely and only using debit cards or cash for all of your purchases can potentially result in a low credit score since you need to build a positive credit history for your credit score to grow over time..
